Hotel rooms are equipped with modern amenities to ensure a comfortable stay, including free Wi-Fi,
flat-screen TVs, and climate control. Guests can also enjoy the convenience of in-room dining, a mini-bar, and a well-appointed en-suite bathroom.

Double Room

A room for two people, equipped with one large bed, typically a double or queen-sized bed. It provides more space and is commonly booked by couples or friends traveling together. Amenities are tailored for two occupants.

Twin room

This room has two single beds, making it suitable for two guests who prefer separate beds. It is often booked by friends, colleagues, or family members who want to share a room but sleep separately.

Single room

This room is designed for one person, offering a single bed. It is typically smaller in size compared to other room types, with amenities suited to a solo traveler. It's ideal for business travelers or those on a budget..


Triple room

This room is suitable for three guests and can have different bedding configurations, such as three single beds, or one double bed and one single bed. It’s ideal for small groups, families, or friends traveling together.

Queen room

A room featuring a queen-sized bed, which is larger than a double but smaller than a king-sized bed. It provides ample space for two people, making it popular among couples and small families.

King room

A luxurious room with a king-sized bed, offering more sleeping space for two guests. It is ideal for those seeking more comfort and room to spread out, commonly available in upscale hotels.

Quadruple room

Designed for four occupants, this room can feature two double beds, four single beds, or a combination of bed types. It is spacious and convenient for families or groups who want to stay together while traveling.
